The tiger count has started in the Sundarbans of West Bengal on Monday. This calculation will start with camera trapping. For this, 1 thousand 464 cameras are being installed in Sundarbans. This camera will take pictures of tiger movement in Sundarbans. The forest department will review the images to determine how many tigers there are in the Sundarbans of West Bengal, India. However, according to the 2022 survey, there are currently 100 Bengal tigers in the Sundarbans.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

50 forest personnel have been given special training at Sajnekhali Forest Department to install trap cameras for tiger census. The installation of the camera started yesterday. And it will end on Thursday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Jones Justin, deputy field director of the Sundarban Tiger Project, told reporters that India has 51 tiger reserves. Sundarban Tiger Reserve is the 31st. There are now 100 tigers in this Sundarbans. Tiger census has been started again yesterday. However, in 1987, the Sundarbans, located on the land of West Bengal and Bangladesh, was declared a World Heritage by UNICEF. With that aim, the tiger census of the West Bengal part of the Sundarbans has also started.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Jones Justin also said that 732 locations have been selected for the census in the Indian Sundarbans and are being installed with sophisticated trap cameras. This automatic camera using infrared technology will take pictures of tiger movement for 35 consecutive days. After 35 days, the camera will be opened and the forest officials will analyze the images captured by the camera to ascertain how many tigers are moving in the forest.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In this Sundarbans of West Bengal, there is the Bengal Tiger, the king of Sundarbans. 100 Bengal tigers and other animals are found here. Although the Bengal tiger lives in India, Bangladesh, Nepal and Bhutan.
